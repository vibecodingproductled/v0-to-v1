## Dimension Key

| Column | Dimension | What "10" looks like |
|--------|-----------|---------------------|
| Opening | Opening / framing | States purpose, sets expectations, grounds in specific real project |
| Questions | Question quality | Open-ended, single-barreled, invites narrative |
| Follow-up | Follow-up depth | Every workaround gets a "why" probe |
| Listening | Listening ratio | Talks ≤20% of the time |
| Leading | Leading question avoidance | No embedded assumptions, no mid-flow rephrasing |
| Tangents | Tangent management | Redirects within ~90 seconds |
| Hypotheses | Hypothesis testing | Pre-session assumptions mapped, post-session assessed |
| Quantify | Pain quantification | Gets specific numbers (time, cost, frequency) |
| Closing | Closing technique | Mirror-back, catch-all, follow-up commitment |
| Grounding | Real-world grounding | Every answer anchored to specific project or incident |
| Bias | Confirmation bias avoidance | Unprompted phases contain zero framework references |

## How to read your tracker

**After 5 interviews**: Look at your worst dimension. That is your priority improvement area.

**After 10 interviews**: Compare your rolling average (last 5) to your first 5. Are you improving, plateauing, or regressing?

**After 20 interviews**: Look for regression under pressure. Do your scores drop when you are running interviews in quick succession (sprint mode)? If so, add buffer days between sessions.

## Trend indicators

- **Improving**: Score trending up by 1+ over rolling 5-interview average
- **Plateauing**: Score within ±0.5 of rolling average for 5+ interviews
- **Regressing**: Score trending down by 1+ over rolling average (often correlates with interview fatigue or time pressure)

## Common patterns

- **Preparation improves, execution degrades**: Better hypothesis-driven guides can paradoxically increase talk time (more complex questions = more explaining). Watch listening ratio when preparation quality goes up.
- **Early interviews score low on bias avoidance**: Normal. This dimension only becomes meaningful after 10+ interviews when you have a framework to be biased toward.
- **Follow-up depth is the hardest dimension to sustain**: Most interviewers plateau here. The standing follow-up to practice: "Why has it stayed that way?"
