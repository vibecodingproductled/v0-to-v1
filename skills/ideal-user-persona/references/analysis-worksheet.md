## Worked example (invented)

**1. Commitment behavior.** Feasibility studies completed per month. Why: completing a study means the product carried a real project decision, unlike logins or views. Mind-changer: if completed studies stop correlating with retention at the next cohort read.

**2. Clusters.** Export of 1,240 active users, clustered on studies/month, return latency after first study, and breadth (how many analysis types touched). Four clusters: Explorers (610, one study then dormant), Checkers (330, monthly single-type studies), Drivers (180, weekly multi-type studies, fast returns), Admins (120, zero studies, user management only).

**3. Concentration.** Drivers: 4.2x the studies/month of Checkers, 2.9x faster return after first value, 41% of all colleague invites from 15% of users. Motivation beats size: Explorers are 3x larger and produce 6% of invites.

**4. Persona.** The early-phase design lead who owns the go/no-go recommendation on 2-4 concurrent projects and must defend it to non-designers. They run every scenario through the product the week a site arrives, and they are the ones pulling consultants into shared projects. Signature: 3+ studies/week in active phases, 3+ analysis types, invites within first month, returns within 48h of new project creation.

**5. Not serving.** Admin-side coordinators, who would need portfolio dashboards we will not build this year. Late-phase detailers, who would need construction-document depth that is a different product.

**6. Growth wiring.** Acquisition: early-phase communities and the search moment "quick feasibility check [site type]". Activation metric: first completed study within 7 days of signup (hypothesis: predicts Driver membership; threshold pre-registered). Expansion: Drivers invite consultants; make sharing the default study output. Pricing: per-active-project scales with Driver success; per-seat punishes the invites we want.
